About

I'm a recent PhD graduate in Computer Science. I did my doctoral studies at the Programming Methods Laboratory (LAMP) at EPFL under the supervision of Professor Martin Odersky.

In my research, I like to explore the many aspects of programming languages. I'm particularly interested in type systems and theory, the semantics and implementation of domain-specific languages, as well as formal methods for scientific modeling.

I am participating in the ongoing effort to formalize Scala's type system. I like to mechanize proofs in Agda, especially type soundness proofs. I'm interested in the design of domain-specific languages for modeling probabilistic and stochastic systems, especially biochemical systems. I have also written a GNU/Emacs mode for Kappa, a modeling language for systems biology.
